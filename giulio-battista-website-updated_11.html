Code System & Analytical Framework
Methodology

Software: Atlas.ti (v25.0.1) for systematic qualitative coding and analysis.

Corpus: 42 official EU documents including EEAS statements, European Commission communications, European Council conclusions, and European Parliament resolutions covering 2004–2025, with a focus on EU–Georgia relations.

Approach: Coding focused on identifying hegemonic discourse patterns, power asymmetries, and strategic framing techniques. The dominant code — "Conditionality and Normative Hierarchies" at 94 instances — accounts for nearly 30% of all coded segments, revealing how EU discourse systematically frames Georgia's European aspirations through conditionality mechanisms while maintaining structural power asymmetries.

Key Finding: The tension between normalisation strategies (51 instances) and explicit Self vs Other differentiation (48 instances) illustrates a contradictory logic: the EU simultaneously naturalises its normative authority while demarcating civilisational boundaries. This contradictory pattern is particularly pronounced in Josep Borrell's statements, which dominate the corpus (22 of 42 documents), revealing the hegemonic dimensions of EU enlargement policy.

Document Sources: European External Action Service (EEAS), European Commission, European Council, and European Parliament — spanning statements, press remarks, resolutions, and policy communications across two decades of EU-Georgia relations.
